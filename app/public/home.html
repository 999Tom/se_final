About Us


St Cuthbert’s Hospice began offering services to the community of Durham in September 1988. Since then we have developed our services to meet growing and changing needs and we have become well-respected for the impact we have had on many lives. We are a registered charity (charity number 519767). Each year we produce our annual report and accounts which are submitted to the Charity Commission. You can see our latest annual report and learn more about our finances here. We are governed by a Board of Directors.

Caring For People Since 1984



The Family Support Team provides counselling, chaplaincy and social work support to In Patient and Living Well Centre guests, their famillies and carers at each stage of their health journey. We also provide an adult counselling and support service to the residents of Durham who have been affected by the diagnosis or death of someone with a life-limiting illness. This can be accessed via a referral from any health professional or you can contact the team direct here at the Hospice. We work with Children and Young People who have experienced bereavement and loss issues and have a specially designed area in the Hospice where our experienced counsellors can help families through the difficult times. This service is open to referrals across the whole of County Durham. Find out more about Children's Berevement Services and The Jigsaw Project. We have “A Time to Reflect” service here at the Hospice three times a year to remember those who have died at the hospice. Families, carer(s) and friends are invited to meet together to celebrate the lives of their loved ones.
